Catalyzing Global Stories in Southeast Asia

In a landmark move to cement Jakarta’s status as a premier global filming destination, the Jakarta Film Commission (JFC) is proud to announce the launch of the "Jakarta Greenlight" International Co-Production Grant for the 2025 fiscal year.

Designed to foster creative collaboration between Indonesian filmmakers and international production houses, the grant offers up to USD 250,000 USD in direct financial support for qualifying projects that showcase the unique urban landscape and cultural richness of the Indonesian capital.

The initiative comes at a time when Southeast Asian narratives are gaining unprecedented traction on the world stage. Jakarta Greenlight aims to reduce the financial barriers for international crews filming in the city while ensuring that local talent—from cinematographers to post-production specialists—is integrated into world-class workflows.

“This is more than just a financial incentive,” says Budi Santoso, Executive Director of the Jakarta Film Commission. “It is an invitation for the world's best storytellers to discover the limitless possibility of our city. From the futuristic glass towers of the CBD to the historic alleys of Kota Tua, Jakarta is a character waiting to be written.”
